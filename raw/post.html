A Name is Just a Name

For the last year and a half, it would have been common to find me at the office for 12-16 hours a day. And for the most part I enjoyed it. The busier I was, the more motivated I was to stay organized and on top of the things that were important to me.
But as everyone knows, you can’t sustain a schedule like that indefinitely. I started missing out on opportunities with friends and colleagues. I became so busy with my office work, that I stopped dreaming up side projects and learning opportunities for myself. I let my label of a “busy guy” take over, and prevent me from understanding what was most important to me, what excited and inspired me, and most importantly, from tracking my progress as a young person in my field.

It was stagnation, where I had never felt stagnated. And it was my own fault. The phrase “under promise, over deliver” is used too often, sure. But lately I’ve been trying very hard to apply it to my own life, specifically to myself and my own expectations.
